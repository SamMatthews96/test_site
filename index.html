About Me

Hello, my name is Sam Matthews. I want to pursue a career in software development because I enjoy solving problems and I gain satisfaction from creating things that other people can use and benefit from. Since September 2019 I have been developing my code abilities, primarily in Python, and I created this page to showcase what I've learned.

Python

I volunteer for DigiLocal, a registered charity that holds free tech clubs for young people, with a focus on supporting those from groups that are under-represented within the tech industry. At DigiLocal clubs children learn to code by creating games and animations in Python and Scratch. As a Digilocal Ambassador I provide supervision and support for the young people as they tackle their projects, assisting them with problem solving and debugging their code. Beyond my usual role I have developed and contributed my own projects to DigiLocal's library and supported DigiLocal teams at the Bristol NASA space apps challenge 2019. I really enjoy helping at DigiLocal as the children find the content engaging and it's an experience that I would have enjoyed as a child.

I also volunteered as a 'Digital Champion' for Leonard Cheshire Disability, a charity that provides many services for people with mental and physical disabilities. People with disabilities have a disproportionately high rate of computer illiteracy, which in addition to their conditions can make interacting with the world especially challenging. My role was to educate clients in a wide range of IT basics including use of a mouse and keyboard, Microsoft Office and safe use of the internet.
